### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The workspace at the Bitexco tower office is modern, tidy, clean, and comfortable, providing a great condition for me to focus on studying and working on projects. What impressed me the most is the spirit of mutual readiness to help among FCAJ program members, even outside official working hours; whenever I messaged to ask about getting stuck in a Lab, I received responses quite quickly.

**2. Support from Mentors / Team Admin**  
The mentors provided very thorough guidance, was willing to re-explain if I didn't understand, and always encouraged me to actively ask questions instead of being afraid of making mistakes. I especially liked how the mentor let me figure out the solution on my own first, only giving hints when I was truly stuck; this approach helped me remember the knowledge much longer compared to being given the answer right away. The members of the FCAJ Team Admin also provided excellent support in terms of procedures and documentation, which saved me a lot of time on administrative tasks and allowed me to focus on studying and working on projects.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
The assigned tasks closely aligned with what I learned at university, particularly in the fields of computer networking and systems. At the same time, it opened up some new knowledge areas that my university curriculum hadn't covered, such as how to operate and secure a real-world Cloud system. As a result, I both reinforced my theoretical foundation and gained practical skills that would be difficult to access if I only studied at school.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
Besides specialized knowledge about AWS, I also learned several crucial soft skills, such as how to present a technical issue coherently, how to write easy-to-understand instruction documents for others, and how to manage time among multiple tasks simultaneously. The program also regularly organized presentation sessions on new technology topics, including AI, which gave me additional perspectives outside the scope of the Labs I was studying and somewhat helped me clarify my future career path.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
The general atmosphere was quite positive; everyone respected each other and worked seriously but without being overly stressed. When someone encountered a difficulty in a Lab or project, others in the team were ready to pause their own work to help, regardless of what learning stage they were at in the program. This made me feel like a true part of the team, even though I was only participating as an intern for a short period of time.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
The program provides an internship allowance and is quite flexible in terms of time, allowing me to proactively schedule my self-study and office hours to match my university schedule. Being able to participate in internal training sessions, including thematic presentations, was also a huge plus, helping me access a lot of content beyond the official learning path.
